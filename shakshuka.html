Shakshuka

This flavorful North African dish features poached eggs in a rich tomato sauce with peppers, onions, and spices. It's perfect for breakfast or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 Onion (chopped)
  • 1 Red bell pepper (chopped)
  • 2 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp Ground cumin
  • 1 tsp Ground paprika
  • 1/2 tsp Ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp Cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 28-oz can Diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 tsp Sea salt
  • 1/4 tsp Black pepper
  • 4-6 large Eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro (for garnish)
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and red bell pepper, cooking for 5-7 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the cumin, paprika, coriander, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, salt, and black pepper. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes, until it thickens slightly.
  5. Use a spoon to make small wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into each well. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny (or cook longer for firm yolks).
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro and crumbled feta cheese (if desired). Serve with warm pita or crusty bread.
